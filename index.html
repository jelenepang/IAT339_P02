BRANDING

THE COMPANY

MODU, as the name may suggest, designs modular furniture. We value function, quality and comfort, targeting small home owners looking to utilize limited spaces to their maximum potential.

With a strong focus on flexibility and as firm believers of sustainable quality, MODU hopes to help home owners find the right furniture for their homes; without the extravagent price tags. This small company is hopeful in brightening limited spaces and teaching customers that smart interior designing is crucial!

Personas

  1. MATILYN is a 23-year-old university student looking to furnish her newly rented apartment. She is in search of space-saving furniture that can maximize the small space she is moving into and needs room to work with when she has guests over. Matilyn is always a careful purchaser of furniture as she expects them to last a lifetime.
  2. CAROL is a single mom looking to furnish her young toddler's room. Because she does not have a big room to work with, she hopes to find multifunctional furniture that can serve multiple needs without taking up too much space.

TYPOGRAPHY

The majority of the MODU website will utilize LATO, a simple san serif font that is not too distracting nor difficult to read on a screen.

TITLE LATO BOLD all caps

Main titles of each page within the website will be of the largest bolded font size and in all capitals.

HEADINGS LATO BOLD all caps

Headings that help organize sections on a page will be bolded but at a smaller font size than titles and in all capitals.

Subheadings LATO BOLD regular case

Subheadings for categorization within sections will be bolded and in regular case.

Body Text LATO REGULAR normal case

Body text for the majority of text content will be styled regularly and using regular casing.

In-text Links LATO REGULAR underlined


in-text links will be regular case and underlined to denote clickability.

COLORS

The website will follow the collection of natural but vibrant colors shown below which includes a neutral, base champagne color, two secondary colors, and an accent color.
Keeping these colors relatively bright help create a youthful and fun look while keeping customers feeling welcomed as they navigate the website.

DARK SAGE #283f21

primary brand color for logo, headlines and main elements on the screen

ROSE #d1765b

accent color for highlighting
important buttons and focused hover states

MOSS #94a776

secondary color for elements lower on the visual heirarchy such as denoting interactivity

TAN #d2b48c

secondary color used mainly for lines and borders

CHAMPAGNE #fffbea

background color acting as base color for the site

ART DIRECTION

Imagery

As an electronic shop geared towards selling product, imagery is mainly reserved for product listings. The site is otherwise kept quite simple with minimal illustrations if necessary for aesthetic purposes.
